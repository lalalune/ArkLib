The key repair is that a close-codeword carrier only has to cover the bad combining scalars for
the current stack. It no longer has to contain every codeword of the ambient code. The in-tree
instance proves the weaker `|F|^n * max(1, 2*delta*n)` bound and regression-checks the interface.

The newer petal-certificate surface connects the generic GKL/GCXK disjoint-petal accounting to
the corrected witness-cover residual: proving a close-codeword carrier plus, for each carried
codeword, a large maximal domain with pairwise-disjoint nonempty petals in its complement now
directly yields the `B_T * p * n` first-moment bound.

The maximal-domain wrapper now also proves the downstream half of the sunflower argument for a
single witness codeword: if a maximal correlated-agreement domain `D` is strictly contained in
each bad line-agreement domain and every pairwise intersection remains large, then the line
petals are pairwise disjoint and the per-codeword bad-scalar count is at most `p * n`.
The new `GKL24MaxCorrWitnessCoverHypothesis` packages the same hypotheses at the carrier level and
instantiates the corrected witness-cover residual with `b = p * n`, including direct count and
`ε_mca` endpoints. It also instantiates the explicit petal-certificate residual by choosing the
canonical `linePetal D` family.

The pairwise large-intersection clause is now a theorem, not a producer obligation, in the
standard parameter range: `lineAgreeSet_inter_card_ge_of_mem_mcaBadWitness` derives it from the
bad-witness agreement-size lower bounds when `2 * delta_mca <= p <= 1`, and
`GKL24MaxCorrWitnessCoverHypothesis_of_strict_cover` lifts the smaller strict-expansion-only
residual to the full max-corr residual.

What is still unproven here is the sharp external theorem instantiating the max-corr residual at
`B_T = L^2` and `p = delta_list` (equivalently the derived first-moment witness-cover residual
with `b = delta_list * n`). That is the remaining GKL24/GCXK25 first-moment content.

## Formal Apex Caveat

Mersenne-521 field instance. This theorem is closed and kernel-clean, but it resolves the
formal/collapsed predicate, not the genuine ABF26 MCA threshold value.

The reason is structural: the produced `GrandMCAResolution` witnesses use `deltaStar = 1`, and
their maximality field is vacuous because there is no `delta` with `1 < delta <= 1`. The genuine
content is the radius-one bound

```text

```

The list side, `prizeResolution_ld`, is genuinely negative and non-vacuous: it refutes the formal
list-decoding prize and proves that the corresponding `GrandListResolution` type is empty under
the stated positivity hypotheses.
