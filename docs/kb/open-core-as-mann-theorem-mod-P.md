# The open core as a "Mann's theorem mod P" for 2-power roots of unity (#407)

A clean geometry-of-numbers localization of the prize's open core, connecting the moment form, the
house/Minkowski form, and classical vanishing-sums-of-roots-of-unity theory. **Not a closure** вҖ” it
restates the open problem in the sharpest, most classical terms found so far, and pinpoints exactly why
the prize regime is the hard one.

## The chain of equalities (all in-tree / proven)

The prize floor `M = max_{bвү 0}|О·_b| вүӨ вҲҡ(2n log m)` follows (moment method, optimized at `r вүҚ log m`) from
the **char-`p` sub-Gaussian moment bound**
```
E_r(Ој_n, F_p)  вүӨ  (2rвҲ’1)!! В· n^r          for r вүҚ log m,   n = 2^Ој,   p вүЎ 1 (mod n).
```
Over в„Ӯ this is the LamвҖ“Leung theorem (proven, all `r`: vanishing sums of `2^Ој`-th roots of unity are
generated by antipodal pairs `О¶^a + О¶^{a+n/2} = 0`, so the only `2r`-tuple coincidences are the
`(2rвҲ’1)!!` perfect matchings). Write the **char-`p` excess**
```
W_r := E_r(Ој_n, F_p) вҲ’ E_r(Ој_n, в„Ӯ)  вүҘ 0.
```
`W_r = 0  вҹә  char-`p` faithful вҹ№ floor holds. So the entire open core is: **bound `W_r` at depth `rвүҚlog m`.**

## `W_r` is a count of short root-of-unity sums in a prime ideal

A char-`p`-only coincidence is a pair of `2r`-tuples with `ОЈ О¶^{a_i} вүЎ ОЈ О¶^{b_j} (mod p)` but not over в„Ӯ,
i.e. a **nonzero** `D = ОЈ_i О¶^{a_i} вҲ’ ОЈ_j О¶^{b_j}` (a `Вұ`-sum of `вүӨ 2r` roots of unity) lying in the
**prime ideal `P = ker(в„Ө[О¶_n] вҶ’ F_p, О¶ вҶҰ h)`**, `h` a primitive `n`-th root of unity in `F_p`. So
```
W_r  =  #{ nonzero Вұ-sums of вүӨ 2r of the n-th roots of unity that lie in P }.
```

## Why the easy proof fails вҖ” and where the threshold is

The tempting closure: `D` is a sum of `вүӨ2r` roots of unity вҹ№ `house(D) вүӨ 2r`; if `D вҲҲ pв„Ө[О¶]\{0}` then
`house(D) вүҘ p`, so `p > 2r вҹ№ D = 0`, no excess. **This is wrong:** the condition is `D вҲҲ P`, not
`D вҲҲ pв„Ө[О¶]`. Because `p` splits completely (`F_p вҠҮ Ој_n`), `P` has **residue degree 1**, `N(P) = p`, so by
Minkowski its **minimum house is `~ p^{1/ПҶ(n)} = p^{2/n}`, not `p`**. Hence:

> **The norm bound proves `W_r = 0` only while `2r < p^{2/n}`, i.e. `n < 2 log p / log(2r) вүҲ 2 log p / loglog p`.**

This is the *small-subgroup* regime. In the **prize regime `n вүҲ p^{1/4}`**, `p^{2/n} вҶ’ 1` (numerically:
`p^{2/n} = 1.19` at `n=256`, `1.016` at `n=4096`, `1.0000` at the prize point `p=2^128, n=2^32`), so the
norm bound is **completely vacuous** вҖ” it forbids nothing. `P` has short vectors of house `вүҲ 1 вүӘ 2r`, so
char-`p` coincidences are *a priori* possible. (`probe_geometry_of_numbers_threshold.py`.)

## The open core, named: "Mann's theorem mod P"

So `W_r` is governed by the *actual* number of short `Вұ`-root-of-unity sums in `P`, and there is a sharp
tension:
- **Volume heuristic (random lattice):** `P` is a rank-`ПҶ(n)` lattice of covolume `~ pВ·вҲҡdisc`; the number
  of `Вұ`-sums of `вүӨ2r` roots of unity it contains is `~ (2r)^{ПҶ(n)} / p`, which is `вү« 1` once
  `n > 2 log p / log(2r)` вҖ” predicting `W_r` *large* (floor false).
- **Measured reality (full enumeration in-regime, `probe_inregime_moment_split.py`):** `E_r/Wick вүӨ 1` and
  *decreasing* in `r` вҖ” `W_r вүҲ 0`, floor holds with margin.

The roots-of-unity arithmetic **suppresses** the count far below the volume heuristic. Over в„Ӯ that
suppression is *exactly* Mann's theorem (minimal vanishing sums of `2^Ој`-th roots = antipodal pairs only).
The open core is the mod-`P` analogue:

> **Prize core вҹә a "Mann's theorem mod `P`":** the only short (`length вүӨ 2r вүҚ log p`) `Вұ`-sums of `2^Ој`-th
> roots of unity lying in `P` are the trivial ones (those already `= 0` over в„Ӯ, i.e. antipodal-generated) вҖ”
> equivalently `P` contains no *short, genuinely-`p`-adic* vanishing relation among `2^Ој`-th roots of unity
> of length `вүӨ 2 log p`.

This is a **closed, classical statement** (no coding theory, no analysis): bound the length of the shortest
nonzero element of `P` that is a `Вұ`-sum of `n`-th roots of unity, for `n = 2^Ој`, `p вүЎ 1 (mod n)`, `n вүҲ p^{1/4}`.
It is equivalent to BCHKS Conjecture 1.12 and to the prize floor. It is **open** вҖ” Mann/LamвҖ“Leung control
the archimedean (`= 0`) relations completely, but there is no known theorem controlling the `p`-adic
(`вҲҲ P`, `вү  0`) short relations once `p^{2/n} вҶ’ 1`. (A "Mann's theorem mod `P`" would close the prize; none
exists.)

## Why this is the right frontier (and what it rules out)

- It is **not** an analytic `вҲҡ`-cancellation statement (so the BGK/di-Benedetto `n^{0.989}` machinery, which
  bounds the sum directly, is the wrong tool вҖ” it can't see the exact suppression).
- It is **not** second-order (so the meta-theorem's `вҲҡp` cap does not apply to *this* statement вҖ” the
  meta-theorem caps methods that use only `ОЈ О·ВІ`; the short-vector count is a genuinely higher object).
- It is a **lattice/Diophantine** question about a single explicit ideal `P` and the explicit finite set of
  `Вұ`-root-of-unity sums вҖ” fully discrete, no asymptotic averaging.

The honest residue: proving the suppression (that the roots-of-unity structure keeps `P` free of short
relations) is the 25-year-open content. This note does not prove it; it states it as cleanly and classically
as the campaign has managed, and identifies the one missing theorem ("Mann mod `P`") by name.
