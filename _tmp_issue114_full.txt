**Anti-vacuity finding on the cross-phase residual** (#121-style audit; I have deep context from the prize-side keystone work). `SpartanBricks.r1csMleEncodingResidual` is documented as *"the single genuinely-mathematical gap between the two sum-check phases"* — but as currently stated it is **vacuous**:

```lean
def r1csMleEncodingResidual : Prop :=
  ∀ stmt oStmt, evalClaimValue R pp stmt oStmt = evalClaimValue R pp stmt oStmt   -- X = X
theorem r1csMleEncodingResidual_holds : … := by intro stmt oStmt; rfl
```

It is a reflexive self-equality, discharged by `rfl`; it captures **none** of the chaining content. The `#print axioms` will be clean precisely because the statement says nothing — the same F4 trap I documented for `prizeResolution_mca_M521` on #121.

**The genuine identity it should state.** From `Spartan/Basic.lean`:
- `zeroCheckVirtualPolynomial 𝒢(Z) = ∑ₓ eqPoly(Z,x)·C(A𝕫(x)·B𝕫(x) − C𝕫(x))` — the MLE of the *pointwise* residual `g(x) = A𝕫(x)·B𝕫(x) − C𝕫(x)`. First sum-check reduces the zero-check to `𝒢(r_x)`.
- `evalClaimValue idx = MvPolynomial.eval r_x (MLE (Mᵢ𝕫 ∘ finFunctionFinEquiv))` = `v_idx`, the MLE of the *row vector* `Mᵢ𝕫` at `r_x`.

The real chaining obligation is **not** `𝒢(r_x) = v_A·v_B − v_C` — that is FALSE in general, because `MLE(A𝕫·B𝕫 − C𝕫)(r_x) ≠ MLE(A𝕫)(r_x)·MLE(B𝕫)(r_x) − MLE(C𝕫)(r_x)` (MLE of a product ≠ product of MLEs). That inequality is *precisely why Spartan needs the second sum-check*: it separately reduces each `v_idx = MLE(Mᵢ𝕫)(r_x) = ∑_y MLE(Mᵢ)(r_x,y)·MLE(𝕫)(y)`. So the honest residual is the **bundled-claim consistency**: the first sum-check's output target (the value the verifier now believes for `𝒢(r_x)`, expressed via the eq-structure at `r_x`) equals the random-linear-combination `∑_idx ρ_idx · v_idx` of the bundled evaluation claims that the second sum-check then opens — with each `v_idx` carrying the genuine MLE matrix-vector encoding above.

**Recommendation**: restate `r1csMleEncodingResidual` as that non-vacuous equality (either prove the MLE evaluation identity `v_idx = ∑_y MLE(Mᵢ)(r_x,y)·MLE(𝕫)(y)`, which IS provable from `MvPolynomial.MLE` linearity + the `eqPolynomial` sum identity already in `RSPhases.lean`/`MLE_eval_boolEmbedding`, or residualize it honestly). As written, #114 cannot be closed on this residual — the "mathematical gap" is still entirely open, just hidden behind a tautology. (The `Nonempty (OracleReduction …)` residuals in the same file — `first/secondSumcheckResidual`, `composedPIOPResidual` — are by contrast honestly stated existence obligations.)

## Addendum: WHY the append-completeness keystone is genuinely deep (the sequencing swap)

A decisive subtlety, documented in `Append.lean` itself (lines ~4117-4128): **`(R₁.append R₂).run = R₁.run >>= R₂.run` is NOT true at the `OracleComp` (free-monad) level.** The appended reduction runs *both provers first, then both verifiers*; the sequential form runs (P₁,V₁) then (P₂,V₂). The `Verifier.append_run` (rfl) and `Prover.append_run_msg` (unconditional for message seams) factorizations feed this, but the reordering is the genuinely-hard step. ### Sharper keystone finding: the completeness-composition obstruction is monad-commutation, not glue

Attempting the de-laundering, I found why `reduction_append_perfectCompleteness` is genuinely deep (and why no probability-free shortcut exists):

`Reduction.run` runs the **full prover then the full verifier**. - There is **no clean structural `Reduction.append_run`** to lean on (it's provably not an `rfl`/monadic identity). `Prover.append_run_msg` + `Verifier.append_run` do **not** compose into a reduction-run factoring without the commutation step.

The required lemma is essentially "independent oracle subcomputations commute under the PMF interpretation," which is **not yet in-tree**.
