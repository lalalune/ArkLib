Proximity Gaps Conjecture Fails Near Capacity over Prime Fields
Antonio Kambiré
April 1st 2026

arXiv:2604.09724v1 [cs.IT] 9 Apr 2026

Abstract
In this report we flesh out a sketch by Krachun and Kazanin to prove that for a certain family
of Reed–Solomon codes, proximity gaps fail at radii that are Op1{ log nq below the capacity rate
of the code, where n is the length of the code.

1

Introduction

The proximity gaps conjecture, introduced in [2], asserts that if many points on an affine line f ` zg
are each close to a Reed-Solomon code, then the line itself must be explained by a nearby codeword
pair, that is, the pair rf, gs is close to the corresponding interleaved Reed-Solomon code, a condition
called correlated agreement. While this phenomenon is well-understood up to the Johnson bound
in settings covered in [2] and subsequent papers, its behavior above the Johnson bound is still less
clear and is the subject of active research.
We give a detailed and self-contained proof of a family of Reed–Solomon codes RSrFp , D, ks for
which proximity gaps fail at radii that are Op1{ log nq below the capacity value 1 ´ k{n, where
n “ |D|. This construction builds on a sketch by Krachun and Kazanin [4], making all bounds and
parameter choices explicit. In concurrent work, Appendix A.5 of [3] used this result to formalize a
conjecture on list- and curve-decodability properties of Reed-Solomon codes over prime fields, up
to the information-theoretic limit.
